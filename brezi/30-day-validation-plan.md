# Brezi — 30-Day Validation Plan
**Locked:** March 22, 2026  
**Positioning:** "The easiest way to get consistently great cold coffee at home"

---

## Segment Definitions

### Primary: Frequent Café Cold Coffee Buyers (Function-Driven)
**Qualifies if ALL of the following:**
- Buys cold brew or iced coffee at a café ≥3x per week
- Spending ≥$15/week on cold coffee
- Primary motivation: quality, consistency, or time — NOT social experience or ritual
- Has expressed frustration with cost, inconsistency, or inconvenience

**Screen out:**
- "I go for the vibe / to get out of the house"
- People whose café habit is primarily social

### Secondary: WFH Heavy RTD Carton Buyers
**Qualifies if:**
- Buys RTD cold brew cartons ≥2x per week
- Spending ≥$30/month on RTD cold coffee
- Frustration with cost, freshness, or consistency

---

## Pre-Defined Numeric Thresholds

| Signal | Threshold |
|---|---|
| CTR (winning ad variant) | ≥2% |
| Click-to-intent (signup/purchase) | ≥20% |
| Message comprehension (interview) | ≥70% accurately explain value unprompted |
| Willingness to pay | ≥50% don't object at retail price |
| Café switching intent | ≥40% would reduce café visits if product performs as described |
| Repeat usage | ≥5 uses within 30 days; ≥60% of new buyers hit this |

**Decision rule:** WTP signal outweighs all others. Curiosity ≠ signal. Only layers 4–5 count as validation.

---

## GTM Structure

### 1. Acquisition — Meta D2C (Top of Funnel)
**Platform:** Instagram primary, Facebook secondary  
**Budget:** ~$400 primary segment / ~$200 secondary segment  

**Audience targeting:**
- Specialty coffee shop followers (Blue Bottle, Stumptown, Philz, Verve, Intelligentsia)
- Cold brew/iced coffee interest clusters
- Behavior: premium beverage buyer, online shopper
- Exclude: home brewing gear enthusiasts (likely hobbyists)

**3 Ad Variants ($100–150 each):**
1. *Economic:* "You're spending $200/month on cold coffee. Here's what it looks like when you stop."
2. *Consistency:* "Every café cold brew tastes a little different. Brezi doesn't."
3. *Friction:* "You could make cold brew at home — if it didn't take 12 hours. Now it doesn't."

### 2. Qualification (Mandatory)
Only count leads who clear BOTH:
- ≥3x/week café cold coffee purchase
- Function-driven motivation (not experience)

All others → tracked separately as "market interest", not "segment signal"

**Landing page screener (before counting as valid lead):**
1. How often do you buy cold coffee or cold brew at a café? → ≥3x/week required
2. What's your main reason for going? → Quality/consistency/convenience = pass; "I like the vibe/getting out" = screen out
3. How much do you spend on cold coffee per month? → Shown price point after answer

### 3. Funnel Separation — Track Independently
| Layer | What it means |
|---|---|
| L1: Ad engagement | Market interest — directional only |
| L2: Waitlist signup | Soft intent — directional only |
| L3: Price-qualified intent | Saw price, still interested — moderate signal |
| L4: Purchase / deposit | Real demand — counts as validation |
| L5: Post-conversion usage | Repeat behavior — counts as validation |

**Only L4 and L5 validate the segment. L1–L3 are leading indicators only.**

### 4. Direct Outreach (Day 1 — Not Fallback)
**Channels:** Reddit (r/coffee, r/coldbrewcoffee), targeted Instagram DM, referrals from existing customers  
**Target:** ≥8 qualified interviews completed in Week 1  
**Screener:** Must meet segment definition before interview is counted  
**Purpose:** Validate WTP, switching logic, repeat usage potential  

---

## Testing Structure

### Week 1–2: Run Both Segments in Parallel
- Launch 3 ad variants
- Begin direct outreach immediately (Day 1)
- Complete ≥8 primary + ≥5 secondary interviews
- Track all 5 funnel layers separately

### Midpoint (Day 14): Cut Decision
- Review: comprehension + L3 intent + L4 conversion across segments
- Cut 1 segment minimum
- Redirect remaining budget to stronger signal
- Document what failed and why

### Week 3–4: Go Deep on Strongest Segment
- Cut 2 underperforming ad variants, double down on winner
- Longer interviews (30 min minimum)
- WTP pressure test: quote actual price, measure drop-off
- Repeat usage tracking with any trial/early users

---

## Day 30: Forced Decision

**Success = validated segment + price acceptance + signs of repeat usage**

**Option A — Commit:** ONE segment + ONE message → define 60-day channel strategy  
**Option B — Not yet proven:** State which threshold(s) failed → revisit value proposition, not messaging or branding

No extensions. Weak signal = value prop or segment problem.

---

## Frozen (Do Not Touch)
- Brand world-building (naming, logo, aesthetic)
- Major content production before proof points validated
- Scaling spend before L4/L5 thresholds hit

## Stays Live
- Product presentation must remain credible and premium enough to support price
- Existing customer repeat usage tracking
