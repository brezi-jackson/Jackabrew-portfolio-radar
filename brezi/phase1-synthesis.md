## 4 Customer Personas

### Persona A — "The Cold Brew Devotee"
- Already making cold brew at home; Brezi solves a real pain (filtration, time, mess)
- Devin: filtering a gallon took 1 hour. Erick: kept breaking carafes
- Daily use, morning routine, functional mindset
- Price anchor: espresso machines (~$350-400 reasonable)
- Quote: *"This machine improved my life a lot"* — Devin

### Persona B — "The Espresso Enthusiast Who Added Cold Brew"
- Full specialty setup: Breville/grinder/AeroPress already
- Attracted because Brezi matches espresso workflow: single-serve, fresh, dial-in, no batch waste
- *"Nothing like make it, keep it, not drink it, throw it away — sad"* — Ganesh
- High lifetime value — will buy accessories, beans, upgrades

### Persona C — "The Convenience Upgrader"
- Was doing iced hot coffee or nothing. Brezi is their first real cold brew
- *"Automated cold brew while I have my shower"* — Jim
- Main friction: no grind guidance → tunneling/extraction issues
- Once working: fully converted, loyal

### Persona D — "The Stomach-Sensitive Coffee Lover" ⭐ (Underserved/Untapped)
- Has GI/acid issues, gave up or limits hot coffee
- Cold brew is a health necessity, not just preference
- Ivan started cold brew specifically for stomach acid reasons; still can't drink hot coffee plain
- *"I can barely drink orange juice. But I can still drink cold brew."* — Ivan
- Marketing angle: Cold brew = smoother, lower acid, easier on your body
- Completely absent from current messaging — huge untapped segment

## What They Love (by frequency)
1. **Speed** — 10-15 min vs 12-24 hours. #1 conversion hook across every channel
2. **Flavor** — smoother, less bitter, less acidic. Especially impressive on light roasts
3. **Build quality & aesthetics** — feels premium, proud to display it
4. **Single-serve / no waste** — fits modern coffee workflow
5. **Magnetic accessories** — drip tray + brew cup, called "clever" repeatedly
6. **Founder access / customer service** — Jackson personally doing Zoom calls is huge. Amazon reviewer Chris specifically praised it. Ivan called Brezi's Kickstarter communication "best ever backed"
7. **The science** — blooming mechanism resonates with coffee geeks

---

## What Frustrates Them (by severity)
1. **Grind guidance is absent** — #1 pain across ALL channels. Amazon -1 star, Jim's tunneling, Ganesh confused, Jan learning curve
2. **Learning curve not communicated** — people expect plug-and-play; first failure = doubt the machine not the grind
3. **Power brick / plug design** — Samantha Amazon review, multiple backer surveys
4. **Distilled water requirement** — casual users hate this barrier
5. **Capacity** — Erick and multiple backers want more volume
6. **Seasonality** — Ganesh explicitly said too cold in winter to drink cold brew
7. **Price ceiling ~$400** — consistent across all interviews; $500 requires new features

## Marketing Insights
- **Discovery path:** Instagram ad → YouTube (Brian Chan critical) → Kickstarter/website. Working — amplify it
- **Ivan found via YouTube ad through Jellop** (Kickstarter marketing platform)
- **Reddit** is where skeptics live. Brezi gets called a rip-off there. Need active presence
- **Biggest conversion blocker:** *"Is this real cold brew or just iced hot coffee?"* — every customer had this doubt
- **Price framing:** Always compare to espresso machines, not drip coffee
- **Word of mouth is strong** — every customer recommended it to friends
- **Founder Zoom calls = superpower** — scalable as content/community
- **Kickstarter transparency = major trust builder** — Ivan + multiple others cited this
- **Cold brew served hot** — underutilized angle. Ivan heats concentrate in a secondary device. *"Best of both worlds — no bitterness, no acid, but warm"*
- **Health angle** — low acid cold brew for sensitive stomachs = untapped segment
