### RISKS TO THIS POSITIONING

1. **The "my wearable is wrong" problem:** If Oura or WHOOP gives a bad reading and Brezi makes the wrong brew, users blame Brezi, not the wearable. The system takes on liability for sensor accuracy it doesn't control. Mitigation: Always surface "based on your [device] reading" in copy. Never claim full certainty.

2. **AI fatigue backlash:** The Protocol Optimizer has been burned before by "AI-powered" products that were glorified IF/THEN logic. If Brezi launches with v1 rules-based logic and someone calls it out publicly, credibility damage is severe in this audience. Mitigation: Don't call it AI in consumer copy. Let the outcome speak.

3. **Delegation resistance:** Some high-performers fundamentally don't want to delegate biological decisions. They want to *make* the decision — they just want better information. These people will resist Brezi's autonomy. Mitigation: Override controls must be prominent, frictionless, and celebrated — not buried in settings. The person who overrides Brezi 40% of the time and rates the outcomes highly is still a perfect customer.
